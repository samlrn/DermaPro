# DermaPro Product Brief

## Problem

Dermatology clinics face costly coverage gaps when providers are absent, on leave, or unable to keep up with demand. Patients wait weeks for care, clinics lose procedure days, and dermatologists have limited visibility into flexible short-term work.

Traditional staffing and locum tenens workflows are slow, relationship-driven, and expensive. The product opportunity is a faster self-serve marketplace purpose-built for dermatology.

## Launch Users

- Clinics: dermatology practices that need qualified shift coverage.
- Dermatologists: licensed providers seeking flexible short-term work.

Patients are intentionally out of MVP scope. Patient-facing features introduce HIPAA, insurance, channel conflict, and three-sided marketplace complexity, so they should wait until the B2B marketplace proves traction.

## MVP Features

- Email/password and Google authentication.
- Signup role selection for clinic or dermatologist.
- Clinic profiles with practice, location, EHR, contact, and specialty focus.
- Dermatologist profiles with bio, photo, licenses, certifications, subspecialties, CV, and credential uploads.
- Shift posting with date, duration, compensation, subspecialty, and EHR familiarity.
- Shift discovery filters by state, date, subspecialty, and pay range.
- Apply/save shift flows for dermatologists.
- Applicant review and booking confirmation for clinics.
- Per-shift messaging unlocked after an application.
- Stripe payment capture, platform fee, payout status, invoices, and receipts.
- Email and in-app notifications for applications, matches, confirmations, and payments.

## Near-Term Roadmap

- Credential vault and automated expiration reminders.
- Clinic-side credentialing checklist per booking.
- Two-way reviews and verified badges.
- Matching recommendations for shifts and providers.
- Recurring dermatologist availability and calendar sync.
- Clinic and dermatologist analytics dashboards.
- Native push notifications once iOS and Android apps are active.

## Key Risks

- Marketplace cold start: recruit dermatologists before or alongside clinics.
- Credentialing complexity: start with upload/checklist, then automate before scaling.
- Subspecialty mismatch: enforce tags and prevent incompatible matches.
- Locum agency competition: win on speed, self-serve UX, and dermatology focus.
- Pricing variability: provide transparent rate guidance and optional bands.
